What it does

When you open an app you've chosen to pause, Pause covers the screen with a slow breathing circle and a short timed pause, and shows how many times you've opened that app today. Only then can you continue — or change your mind. It's gentle friction, not a hard block: enough to turn an automatic tap into a deliberate choice.

Built-in screen-time tools

Google's Digital Wellbeing launched on Pixel and Android One devices with Android 9 Pie (2018). Since September 2019, Google has required all GMS-certified devices launching on or upgrading to Android 9 or later to ship a digital-wellbeing solution — so most phones sold after that point include app timers, Focus Mode (Android 10+), and a Bedtime/Wind Down mode. Samsung's One UI ships its own equivalent under "Digital Wellbeing and parental controls."

Pause is aimed at people these tools don't reach:

Older devices

Devices running Android 8 (Oreo) or earlier predate Digital Wellbeing entirely.

Custom ROMs

AOSP-based ROMs such as LineageOS don't ship the proprietary Google Wellbeing app.

Non-GMS devices

Some budget or regionally-sold phones are not Google-certified and ship without GMS apps, including Digital Wellbeing.

Lighter alternative

Even where built-in tools exist, Pause is a single-purpose, open-source option that needs no Google account.

Other apps

Several apps offer similar friction — delaying or blocking access to chosen apps. Many bundle extra features alongside the core mechanism: habit trackers, usage leaderboards, browser filters, or parental controls. Payment models vary too: subscriptions, one-time purchases, or feature tiers that can be hard to compare. Pause does exactly one thing — a calming pause before an app opens — and nothing else. It is free, open-source, and has no payment, no account, and no subscription of any kind.

Set up

  1. Download the latest APK and open it on your phone (allow installs from unknown sources if asked).
  2. Open Pause and turn on accessibility access.
  3. Set your pause length and message, then choose the apps to pause.
